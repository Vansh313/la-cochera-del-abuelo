La Historia

A coach house that became a bar, that became six tables where Seville quietly eats better than almost anywhere else in the city.

In 2016, Cinta Romero took over what was once a tiny neighbourhood bar called El Abuelo, tucked into a quiet street of the Casco Antiguo. She kept the name, the antique china, and the feeling of walking into somebody's home — and built a kitchen around it.

In 2019, chef Bosco Benítez joined her, bringing a French-trained hand to Andalusian ingredients. What started as weekend tasting menus grew into daily service, without ever adding a table. The room still seats barely a dozen — every plate is still finished within a few metres of where it's eaten.
